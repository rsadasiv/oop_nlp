The Xperia XZ1 comes with a metal unibody design, Android 8.0 Oreo out of the box, Sony's Motion Eye camera and Sony's new 3D creator feature that allows users to create 3D scans of objects.

On the other hand, the Sony Xperia XZ Premium features a 5.5-inch 4K IPS LCD display with a 3840x2160 pixel resolution. It sports Corning Gorilla Glass 5 for protection. It is powered by a Qualcomm Snapdragon 835 processor, paired with 4GB of RAM. Running Android Nougat, the device includes an internal storage of 64GB which can be further expanded up to 256GB via microSD card.
It comes equipped with a 19MP rear camera with f/2.0 aperture and LED flash along with a 13MP front-facing camera. Connectivity features include 4G VoLTE, Wi-Fi 802.11ac, Bluetooth 5.0, NFC, GPS/ A-GPS, USB Type-C (3.1) and a 3.5mm audio jack. A 3230mAh battery completes the package.
