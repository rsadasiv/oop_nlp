Honor, Huawei’s sub brand, has launched the Honor 8C in China. The 4GB RAM/32GB storage variant is priced at CNY 1,099 (approximately Rs 11,800) while the price for the 4GB RAM/64GB storage variant is priced at CNY 1,399 (approximately Rs 15,000). It will be available in Aurora Blue, Magic Night Black, Platinum Gold, and Nebula Purple colour variants. The device will go on sale on October 16. At the moment, there is no information provided as to when the company will launch the smartphone in India.
In terms of specifications, the device features a 6.26-inch HD+ TFT LCD IPS display with an aspect ratio of 19:9 and a 720x1520 pixels resolution. It is powered by an octa-core Qualcomm Snapdragon 626 SoC with 4GB of RAM. Running Android 8.1 Oreo with EMUI 8.2, the device is offered in two storage variants – 32GB and 64GB and can be further expanded up to 256GB via microSD card.
The device comes equipped with a dual rear camera set up - 13MP primary sensor along with a 2MP secondary sensor. It also features an 8MP front-facing camera. Connectivity features include 4G VoLTE, single-band Wi-Fi 802.11 b/g/n, Bluetooth v4.2 LE with aptX and GPS/A-GPS. A 4000mAh battery completes the package.
Recently, the Honor Play smartphone went on sale in India. In terms of specifications, the device features a 6.3-inch full-HD+ display with a 19.5:9 aspect ratio and a 1080x2340 pixels resolution. It is powered by a HiSilicon Kirin 970 SoC with two RAM variant options – 4GB and 6GB. Running Android 8.1 Oreo with EMUI 8.2, the device includes an internal storage of 64GB and can be further expanded up to 256GB via microSD card.
The device comes equipped with a dual rear camera set up - 16MP primary sensor along with a 2MP secondary sensor. It also features a 16MP front-facing camera. Connectivity features include 4G VoLTE, Wi-Fi 802.11ac, Bluetooth v4.2, USB Type-C (v2.0), GPS/ A-GPS, and a 3.5mm headphone jack. A 3750mAh battery completes the package.
